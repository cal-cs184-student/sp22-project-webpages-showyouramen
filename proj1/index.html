Overview

In this project, we applied the theory that we learned in lecture/discussion regarding to Rasterization, Antialiasing, Sampling, Tranforms, and Texture-Mapping to a more tangible setting. In essence, we learned how an image could be displayed on a computer screen, given that not all images are of the same dimensions or resolution as the screen provided. The direct application of mathematical theory such as homogeneous coordinates, baycentric coordinates, bilinear interpolation, etc. to actual, viewable results in displayed images was both interesting and rewarding.

Section I: Rasterization

Part 1: Rasterizing single-color triangles

Rasterization is the process where objects (generally given as functions or exact coordinates) are converted to their depictions in a framebuffer. In our case, we are rasterizing triangles by using three line tests, since triangles are made up of three lines. If these three lines bound our pixel-of-interest, we color this pixel in. The way we determine which pixels to be interested in is by first forming a bounding box around our triangle, based on the provided coordinates, and then iterating through the pixels in the bounding box.
